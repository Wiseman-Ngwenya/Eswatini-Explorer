Welcome to Eswatini

A hidden gem in Southern Africa

Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) is a small, landlocked kingdom in Southern Africa known for its wilderness reserves and festivals showcasing traditional Swazi culture. Marked by mountainous landscapes, safari parks, and lush valleys, Eswatini offers an authentic African experience away from the crowds.

From the majestic Sibebe Rock to the wildlife-rich Hlane National Park, our guide will help you discover the best places to visit, stay, and explore in this beautiful kingdom.

Things to Do

Adventure, culture, and relaxation in Eswatini

Hiking & Trekking

Explore trails at Sibebe Rock, Malolotja Nature Reserve, and Mahamba Gorge. Guided hikes available.

  • Difficulty: Easy to Challenging
  • Duration: 2-8 hours
  • Cost: E50-E300 per person

Game Drives

Spot the Big 5 at Hlane National Park or enjoy wildlife viewing at Mlilwane Sanctuary.

  • Best Time: Early morning or late afternoon
  • Duration: 2-3 hours
  • Cost: E150-E400 per person

Cultural Experiences

Visit traditional villages, watch Swazi dancing, and learn about local crafts and customs.

  • Where: Mantenga Cultural Village
  • Duration: 1-2 hours
  • Cost: E80-E150 per person

Craft Market Shopping

Buy authentic Swazi crafts at Ezulwini Valley markets and Mbabane Craft Market.

  • Best Buys: Sisal baskets, wood carvings, candles
  • Bargaining: Expected (25-30% discount)
  • Markets: Open daily 8am-5pm

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Eswatini trip

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (May-September): Best for wildlife viewing and hiking. Cooler temperatures.

Wet Season (October-April): Lush landscapes, fewer tourists, but some roads may be difficult.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors don't need a visa for stays up to 30 days. Check with your embassy for current requirements.

Currency: Lilangeni (SZL), tied to South African Rand (ZAR). Both accepted.

Climate

Getting Around

Car Rental: Available at airports and major cities. Drive on left side.

Taxis: Use registered taxis. Agree on fare before departure.

Local Transport: Khumbis (minibus taxis) are cheap but crowded.

Safety & Health

Eswatini is generally safe for tourists. Take normal precautions in cities at night.

Malaria: Low risk in most areas, but prophylaxis recommended for lowveld regions.

Water: Tap water is generally safe in cities, bottled water available everywhere.

Language & Culture

Official Languages: Swati and English (widely spoken)

Greeting: Handshake is common. Use right hand or both hands as a sign of respect.

Dress Code: Modest dress appreciated, especially in rural areas and cultural sites.
